The College

PSG College of Technology (PSG CT), established in 1951 by PSG & Sons’ Charities, is an ISO 9001:2015 certified autonomous institution affiliated with Anna University, Chennai. This year marks a significant milestone as PSG College of Technology celebrates its Platinum Jubilee, coinciding with the Centenary Year of PSG & Sons’ Charities, the founding trust renowned for its contributions to education and industry. PSG CT offers 21 undergraduate and 24 postgraduate programmes across Engineering and Technology, Computer Applications, Management Sciences, and Basic and Advanced Sciences. The institution is home to several state-of-the-art Centers of Excellence including the TIFAC-CORE in Product Design, Machine Tool Research Centre, Engineering Design Centre, Virtual Reality Centre, Tool and Die Centre, Centre for Nanotechnology, Centre for Robotics, Centre of Excellence in Artificial Intelligence and Software, Centre for Non-Linear Dynamics, Danfoss Centre of Excellence in Climate and Energy, and the Centre of Excellence in Welding Engineering and Technology. Under the PSG Industrial Institute, the college also operates in-campus manufacturing units producing machine tools, pumps, and motors, along with off-campus foundry units, enabling practical industry exposure for students. PSG CT has built a strong network with industries, research organizations, alumni, and entrepreneurs, fostering innovation and collaboration. The institution has received national recognition, ranking second in the ATAL Ranking of Institutions on Innovation Achievements (ARIIA) under the Government and Government-Aided Technical Institutions category by the Ministry of Education, Government of India in 2021, and being awarded the Best Industry-Linked Institution by AICTE-CII in 2012. Further, during India’s G20 Presidency in 2022, PSG CT was chosen as one of 75 leading educational institutions in the country to conduct special lectures, student exchange programmes, and academic and cultural activities to promote awareness of the G20 agenda among students and the academic community.

The Department

The Department of Civil Engineering at PSG College of Technology, established in 1953, has a distinguished legacy of excellence in education, research, and consultancy. The department offers an undergraduate B.E. program in Civil Engineering, M.E. in Structural Engineering, and Ph.D. research opportunities, fostering comprehensive learning and innovation. With a dedicated team of 20 highly qualified faculty members with strong academic backgrounds, the department emphasizes academic rigor, practical training, and industry relevance. The department is actively involved in sponsored research and consultancy projects, supported by advanced computational and experimental laboratories. Over the years, it has organized several prestigious conferences and workshops, including EQADS 2011 (Earthquake Analysis & Design of Structures), SCACT 2012 (Sustainability Challenges and Advances in Concrete Technology), and RCMS 2023 (Recent Advances in Construction Materials and Structures), along with various AICTE-sponsored refresher courses and faculty development programs. The department maintains strong ties with industry and academic institutions worldwide, promoting leadership development, innovation, and knowledge exchange. Many students pursue higher education in reputed universities abroad, and the alumni of the department occupy leading roles in academia, research, and industry, contributing significantly to societal and infrastructural progress.

The Conference

In recent decades, growing concerns over global warming and environmental degradation have brought sustainability to the forefront of discussions across science and engineering. With rapid urbanization and population growth, the demand for housing, industrial development, civil infrastructure, and energy continues to rise. The building and construction sector plays a pivotal role in this scenario—supporting progress while also contributing significantly to climate change and resource consumption. While considerable progress has been made in sustainable construction practices, vast opportunities remain for innovation, especially in the development of eco-friendly materials and efficient structural systems. Advances in science and technology are now enabling smarter interactions between buildings, structures, and their environments, offering new pathways for achieving sustainability goals. The International Conference on Recent Advances in Construction Materials and Structures (RCMS 2026) will serve as a premier forum for researchers, academicians, practicing engineers, industry experts, and students to present and discuss state-of-the-art developments in both materials and structural engineering. The conference will cover a broad spectrum of topics, including innovative materials for construction, green technologies, resilient and adaptive structural systems, and performance-based design approaches. By bringing together expertise from materials science, structural engineering, and construction technology, RCMS 2026 aims to foster collaboration, knowledge sharing, and the development of sustainable solutions for the construction industry of the future.

The Pre-Conference Workshop

“Sustainable Construction: Innovations in Materials and Performance-Based Structural Design” This pre-conference workshop aims to provide participants with comprehensive insights into the latest advancements in sustainable construction materials and modern structural design practices. The focus will be on integrating innovative materials and resilient structural systems to meet the demands of sustainability and durability in the built environment. The workshop will emphasize practical applications, emerging technologies, and the transition from research to real-world implementation. It will feature expert lectures, technical discussions, interactive sessions, and demonstrations that highlight best practices for sustainable construction.

Paper Submission

Researchers and industry professionals are invited to submit abstracts not exceeding 250 words.
All abstracts will be peer-reviewed, and upon acceptance, participants will be required to submit a full-length paper (maximum four pages, including references).
All full-length papers will undergo plagiarism screening prior to technical review. The acceptable similarity thresholds are ≤ 15% for research papers and ≤ 20% for review articles.

Conference Themes

Innovative approaches to structural performance

  • Advanced materials for enhanced structural capacity (e.g., UHPC, FRP, smart materials)
  • Performance-based design and assessment techniques
  • Seismic performance enhancement strategies
  • Structural health monitoring and damage detection systems
  • Sustainable design for long-term performance
  • Prefabricated and modular construction innovations
  • Integration of smart sensors and IoT for real-time performance tracking
  • Composite behaviour
  • Optimization in Structural design(topology shape and size)
Resilience based special elements and structures

  • Energy dissipation and base isolation systems
  • Redundancy and robustness in structural systems
  • Resilient high-rise and long-span structures
  • Structural elements for post-disaster functionality and rapid recovery
  • Multi-hazard risk assessment and mitigation
  • Retrofitting techniques for improving resilience
  • Use of composite and hybrid materials for resilient design
  • Design of resilient infrastructure for extreme loadings (earthquakes, floods, blasts)
Computational tools & Artificial Intelligence in Structural Engineering

  • Finite element modeling and simulation of complex structures
  • Nonlinear static and dynamic analysis
  • Machine learning for predictive maintenance and structural assessment
  • Digital twin technologies in structural engineering
  • AI-driven design automation and optimization
  • Probabilistic and stochastic methods in Structural engineering
  • Integration of BIM (Building Information Modeling) with AI tools
  • Uncertainty quantification and reliability analysis using AI

Durability, Service Life, and Corrosion Mitigation

  • Degradation Mechanisms - sulphate/acid attack, chloride ingress, carbonation, leaching
  • Corrosion Mechanisms in Concrete and Steel
  • Material Behavior – creep, shrinkage, fatigue, fracture
  • NDT & Structural Health Monitoring
  • Reliability and Life-Cycle Resilience
Smart and Functional Construction Materials

  • Cements and secondary binders (SCMs)
  • Special Concrete (FRC, TRC, HPC, UHPC, SHC)
  • Innovative Construction Techniques (Precast, 3D Printing)
  • Chemical Admixtures
  • Metallic and non-metallic reinforcement
  • Alternative Aggregates

Testing, Characterization, and Performance Evaluation

  • Microstructural & Material Characterization
  • Mechanical Performance: Strength, Fatigue & Fracture
  • Durability & Corrosion Assessment
  • Non - destructive Testing & SHM Techniques
  • Extreme Loading Performance: Seismic, Fire & Impact
  • Accelerated Testing & Service Life Prediction
Recycling and Reuse in Pavement Engineering

  • Pavement materials characterisation
  • Design of recycled pavements
  • Recent advances in recycling techniques
  • Recycling agents and their application
